<p><p>Glasses-free 3D TV technology has evolved in two main market types: Active 3D TV and Passive 3D TV. Active 3D TV uses battery-powered glasses that sync with the TV to create a 3D effect through rapid shuttering of the lenses. On the other hand, Passive 3D TV utilizes polarized glasses, similar to those used in movie theaters, to create the 3D effect. Both market types offer viewers a glasses-free 3D viewing experience, but each utilizes different technology to achieve this result.</p></p>
