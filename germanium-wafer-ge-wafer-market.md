<p><p>Germanium wafers are classified based on their conductivity type, either P-type or N-type. P-type Germanium wafers have positively charged "holes" that carry electric current, while N-type Germanium wafers have electrons as the charge carriers. P-type wafers are commonly used in electronic devices like transistors, while N-type wafers are utilized for applications like photovoltaic cells. Each type of wafer offers different electrical properties and is chosen based on the specific requirements of the device being manufactured.</p></p>
