<p><p>Computer paper comes in two main market types: non-perforated and perforated. Non-perforated paper is designed for continuous form printing where the paper feeds through the printer without tearing at the edges. Perforated paper, on the other hand, has dotted lines that allow for easy tearing to create separate sheets. Non-perforated paper is commonly used for high-volume printing tasks, while perforated paper is more convenient for producing individual documents such as invoices or receipts.</p></p>
