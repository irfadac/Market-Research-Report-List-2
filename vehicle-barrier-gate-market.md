<p><p>Vehicle barrier gates come in different types, with the most common being straight and crank gates. Straight barrier gates are simple and operate in a linear motion, moving up and down to allow or restrict access. Crank barrier gates, on the other hand, use a rotating crank mechanism to lift the gate arm. Both types serve the same purpose of controlling vehicle access, but the design and operation differ based on the specific needs of the site.</p></p>
