<p><p>Audio attenuators are used to reduce the amplitude of an audio signal, and are available in two main types in the market: Adjustable and In-line. Adjustable attenuators allow users to manually adjust the level of attenuation, providing more control over the signal. In-line attenuators are fixed levels of attenuation that are integrated into the signal path, providing a consistent level of reduction. Both types are commonly used in audio systems to adjust volume levels and prevent distortion or overload in audio equipment.</p></p>
