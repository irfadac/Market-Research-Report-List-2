<p><p>Enzymes are biological molecules that act as catalysts in various biochemical reactions. There are six main types of enzymes: Oxidoreductases, Transferases, Hydrolases, Isomerases, Lyases, and Ligases. Oxidoreductases catalyze oxidation-reduction reactions, Transferases transfer functional groups between molecules, Hydrolases catalyze hydrolysis reactions, Isomerases catalyze isomerization reactions, Lyases catalyze the removal of groups from substrates, and Ligases catalyze the joining of molecules. Each type of enzyme plays a crucial role in facilitating specific types of biochemical reactions.</p></p>
