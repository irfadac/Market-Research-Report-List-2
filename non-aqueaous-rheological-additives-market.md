<p><p>Non-aqueous rheological additives are categorized into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic rheological additives are derived from natural sources and include substances like cellulose derivatives and acrylic polymers. These additives are known for their compatibility with organic systems and ability to improve viscosity and stability. Inorganic rheological additives, on the other hand, are mineral-based and include substances like clays and silica. They are valued for their high temperature stability and resistance to shear forces in non-aqueous systems.</p></p>
